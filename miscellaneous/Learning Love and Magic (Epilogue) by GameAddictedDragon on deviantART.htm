Centuries Later...

The kingdom of Arendelle had been most successful, never being dominated by war, never experiencing famine, neither drought nor flood. Nobody knew how the kingdom was so prosperous. Some felt the ancient royal bloodline held the answer, and thus the citizens vowed to fight tooth and nail to keep the family on the throne.

However, some claim that at night, at certain times, one can see tiny flashes of light from their periphery. Those people claim the lights held the clue to their good fortune.

Upon the throne was a single queen, as beautiful as she was fair, never making an unfair ruling. And by her side was her little sister, the princess, with whom the queen was rather close.

The queen was blessed with magic, of which she had a hard time controlling.

One evening, during an evening ball, an abnormally tall stranger clad in a cloak approached the queen. The guards stood between the visitor and their queen.

"Why are you here," the queen asked. "Why do you disrupt this happy occasion?"

"I come bearing an offer you simply cannot refuse, Your Majesty: to teach you to better control your magic," the stranger said, removing their hood.

Wait, that seems so... familiar... I've read that somewhere, in a journal, I think, the queen thought to herself, her head swimming.

"Look," the princess said, astonished.

Both the queen and the stranger looked at where the princess was pointing: it was an ancient tarp, centuries old, depicting one of great importance: an elf, juxtaposed over the forms of a gryphon, a dragon, a hawk, and a blood hound. Looming above them all was a dark monstrous creature, an image that used to give the queen nightmares, but then had become a sort of admirable creature to her.

The queen gasped. The elf on the tarp bore an uncanny resemblance to this stranger: pure white hair, dark mocha skin, bright wine eyes. Even the cloak was the same.

"Dominica," the queen whispered.

The elf smiled at the sisters kindly. "Really. It's good to be home."

~~~Farewell...~~~

Elsanna Week 1 Day 1 - Cuddle

Wind sang in curls beyond the painted glass, churning brilliant patterns of greens and blue across Elsa’s icy carpet. Frost crept further along the rugs and wooded floor, coming to a halt at the foot of the walls. Elsa stared icy daggers at the defiant wall, willing her ice to seek dominance over the slaves of her room, but the ice would push no further. After a moment of tight brows and clenched fists, the heiress relaxed her body in a heavy sigh.

Her bed was now frozen nearly solid. She would need to have the sheets replaced in the morning. Maybe the frame, too. The blankets covering her glistened in a way only ice could, without plan but in natural harmony. Elsa wiped a tiny finger over the edge of her sheet, the coarse ice cracking under the pressure.

A knock, or more a series of knocks Elsa had all but memorized, startled her, making her puncture the ice on her bed. She hissed quietly, removing her appendage from the shattered blanket.

“Elsa?” A tiny voice, muffled by the heavy door, whispered into the keyhole. Moving silently, Elsa extricated herself from her frozen confines, padded across her room, and slid to a halt at the base of her door all while avoiding eyesight of the keyhole.

She pressed her hand against the door, her knees pulled to her chest, icing the door. She quickly pulled back her hand, her eyes wide. She waited a moment. The voice returned rising Elsa’s heartbeat with it.

“Elsa?” the voice repeated, though softer than before. “I hope I didn’t wake you,” the soft creature breathed through the keyhole. Elsa flexed her free hand, her other pulling at the hem of her nightgown. “The sounds outside scared me.” Elsa reached for the door swiftly, then pulled her hand back just as fast, eyes wide. She coveted the hand in her lap, her eyes looking up at the keyhole. The angle she was sitting in granted her vision only of the hole itself, but she knew who was sitting outside her door speaking lullabies.

“I thought they woke you too so I just thought I’d come here and, maybe, sleep toferbur…” the last word drifted into gibberish, but it was enough to bring Elsa to her feet. She stared at the door, ice creeping from her toes. She ignored the frost, icy orbs still glued to the wooden entrance.

“You never open your door, Elsa,” the voice continued. Elsa heard a shifting sound, like fabric against her door, then it was replaced by the voice once more. “Why is that?” Ice crept further towards the doors. “We used to get up every night. I’d jump on your bed and take you downstairs and-” the voice stopped abruptly. Elsa closed her eyes. She heard ice pushed further, crackling on wooden planks. “Go outside into the snow,” the voice finished. Elsa released the death-grip on her palms, though the pigment of her skin remained unaltered.

“But I wanna sleeeeeep,” the soft voice moaned through the keyhole. Elsa could see wisps of breath swirling through the air of her frigid room before dissipating a second later. She balled her fists again. “Elsaaaa, I’m sleeeepyyyyyy.” Elsa waited a whole minute, not another word coming from the door, before she heard a thud come from beyond it. In an instant, she rushed to the door, froze the lock to point of corrosion, and swung the door aside.

Beneath her, huddled in a tiny quilt, was the second princess of the house of Arendelle and Elsa’s sister, Anna. Her eyes were shut, but not tight. Elsa could see the centers of her eyes moving beneath the thin membrane that hid them from the outside. Elsa felt her heart stop. Her sister was calm, unmoving, dark circles under her eyes. Her hair was down, pooled across the carpet, void of the braids that normally encased them. A single shaft of white hair streaked down the left side of her hair in stark contrast with the hazelnut of the rest of her head. It was the same shade as Elsa’s unkempt locks.

The soft breaths eschewing from the tiny body’s lips misted, the frigid air of Elsa’s room invading the grand hallway. Elsa stepped into the hallway, her bare feet mingling with the warm carpet. She stood there for a moment, her eyes shut and the tips of her mouth twitching upwards. The air was cooling, but was still far warmer than that of her room. With her eyes closed, it was pitch black, unlike the constant lights glinting in her vision inside her bed. A soft groan from below her brought her out of her revelry. Anna had moved onto her side. Elsa smiled at the small girl, her eyes softening.

Shutting the door quietly, Elsa sucked in some air and hoisted the small creature into her arms, quilt and all. Stubby legs dangled out below Elsa’s arms and a heaving chest pulsed into her shoulder. Blood rushed to Elsa’s cheeks. She turned her eyes to the ceiling and marched dutifully down the hallway.

The castle of house Arendelle was finely built, not a single board or stone creaking or cracking as Elsa walked over the massive rugs that were placed down the halls. Portraits towered over the two sisters, their gazes unreadable in the darkness far above. Winds howled outside, audible even from where Elsa walked. Their howls were sucked by the vast castle, eaten and swallowed by the black void of silence. The castle was vacant, all but empty in the eyes of the older child.

Only a few steps more and she was standing in front of her sister’s room. The door was bright. It was identical to every other door in the keep, but it held an aura that made it brilliant. More brilliant then the lights dancing on Elsa’s icy floor. Taking a deep breath, Elsa pushed open the door.

She always stood here whenever she was about to enter her baby sister’s room. She would stand here, absorbing all the contents within, painting a picture she would keep in her mind until her next visit. This time, she noticed the covers were on the floor, thrown haphazardly in Anna’s quest for a companion. Dolls and clothes were strewn about the room, missing from their spots Elsa last remembered them being.

Elsa released a breath she thought she’d already released. She entered the room, sister slung over her shoulder and door closed, and strode to the side of the bed. With gentle hands, she placed her sister on the bed, replaced the covers, and tucked her in. Anna had made nary a peep or a sigh through the whole process. Elsa leaned on her elbows on the bed, gazing upon her sister’s face. Her skin shined in the gentle light flowing freely from her window, unhindered by any designs in the glass. The blizzard raging beyond did little to hide the child’s features. Her hair was pulled behind her, splayed out over the top of the covers. Eyes rested lazily, irises flicking incessantly against the backs of her eyelids. Her mouth laid agape, drool forming at the bottom of her lips. Elsa pushed at her face when her cheeks began to hurt.

She dabbed at Anna’s mouth with her dress and pushed some stray hairs from her unblemished face. Elsa looked at her, a thought contorting her previously smiling visage. Her eyes scanned the prone body, taking in every minute detail. Wind beat the castle walls outside, demanding entry into the stubborn walls, as Elsa fought her bodiless enemy.

Sighing, she opened the sheets covering her sister. Anna curled, recoiling at the change in temperature. Elsa was quick to close the gap and reseal the bed. She scooted in closer to Anna. Elsa could feel the younger girl’s breath mingling with her own. She gasped, reaching behind her. She closed her eyes and withdrew her arm, shaking her head. Elsa pressed in close, wrapping her pale arms around her sun-kissed sister. Her body was hot, a far cry from the world Elsa typically surrounded herself with. The breath on her neck tingled, warming her entire head. She exhaled sharply, allowing herself to sink into the large pillows cradling their heads.

Her fingers clutched at her sister’s back, holding her in close. With every breath, Elsa was urged to pull her in tighter. The mattress beneath them dipped as their bodies met in the middle. Her face was buried in Anna’s hair, the scent filling her nostrils. She inhaled deeply, squeezing the soft flesh of Anna’s arms.

Elsa lay next to her sister for minutes, or hours, the winds coursing over and around the window in the corner of the room. The light shifted, though Elsa’s closed eyes didn’t take notice. She clutched her sister as though she were her lungs and heart wrapped in her protective ribs.

Anna twitched. Elsa’s eyes were barley open by the time she had launched herself from the bed and dove beneath the edge. She waited a minute, expecting a response, and rose upon receiving none. Anna was still asleep, her eyes sweeping in rapid strokes. Elsa sighed, but did not return to her sister’s side.

She busied herself with replacing everything out of place in Anna’s room. Toys were returned to their shelves and clothes neatly folded in Anna’s drawers. She left the curtain open, however, hoping the sun would warm her sister come morning. She cast a smile to Anna’s resting form, the corners of her eyes welling with moisture. She went beside the bed, leaned in, and placed a tiny kiss on Anna’s exposed cheek. When she withdrew, her lips were replaced with a drop of salty water. Elsa looked at, nearly snatching it from Anna’s skin, but stepped away. One final smile, and she left the room.

The door shut as quiet as a coffin, leaving a flawless room in its. No steps were hear trailing down the hall, no raps upon the door or heavy sighs through a keyhole. Only the blizzard and its yowls echoed through the dormant fortress. Anna lay prone in her bed, beneath the raging gale and the heavy stones, her eyes still.

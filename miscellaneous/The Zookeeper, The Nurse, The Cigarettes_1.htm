The Zookeeper, The Nurse, The Cigarettes

Part 1 of a mini-Elsanna tumblr fic

It doesn’t take long to find a parking space.

Elsa applies the handbrake and turns the engine off a little harshly. Her orange Fiat 128 splutters and dies. The radio, which had been blasting some strange remixed version of Wannabe by the Spice Girls, also cuts out.

It’s idiotic; absolutely daft that she would be angry at ample parking. Elsa is anal retentive for being on time after all. The newly installed digital clock on the dashboard of her car reads 1:15 with pride. She’s forty give minutes early. Normally this would be cause for a celebratory pat on the back.

Damn fucking empty car park.

Elsa sighs as she slides out of the car, keys and phone and pack of cigarettes in hand; stuffing the two items that aren’t detrimental to her health in the pocket of her leather jacket whilst simultaneously trying to fish out her lighter. Leaning against the door, Elsa tucks one of her beloved Marlboro’s between her lips and cups two hands around the small flickering flame.

She smokes quietly, watching crows huddle on a fence across the road.

It’s a cold October morning. The chilly air pricks at her un-gloved hands and causes her cheek to pink acutely. The contrasting burn in her lungs is welcome. The cancer cells she is carelessly helping to kill her are not. Which is why she’s here, Elsa muses. Her eyes dart to the building in the distance with those wide glass double doors all doctors’ practices seem to favour.

She waits until the burn hits the foot of the cigarette before walking over to mush it against the ashtray of the nearest trash can. When she looks up from that particular action she realizes that the nearest trashcan? Yeah, it’s at the entrance of the practice.

Fuck.

A mother with a snotty crying child holds the door open for her, looking dog-tired and with strange murky stains on her blouse. And well fuck; now Elsa has to go inside. She tries to smile but knows that she must look more pained than grateful as she accepts the door from the woman with a raspy and altogether unbelievable thanks.

She slinks inside the entryway and follows the mother with the still snotty but now largely subdued child to the front desk. He’s sucking at an orange lollipop absentmindedly while his mother leans all of her weight against the high desk and explains her plight to the secretary. Elsa waits her turn, shifting awkwardly from foot to foot and pretending to become engrossed in the medical posters on the walls highlighting the danger of STD’s.

Good God, what if she has an STD?

She tries to count back the weeks since she had unprotected sex in her head and thankfully comes up empty. Oh … no. Scratch that. August 25th. It was karaoke night with the guys from work; too many beers, and one hot waitress in a red cocktail dress. Shit.

Elsa is sweating buckets by the time the secretary has shown Mother and Snotty Kid to the waiting room. The man waves at her gaily then with a bright and cheery “Yoohoo!” when she shuffles up, worrying herself silly about Syphilis.

He’s huge. His biceps strain against the maroon scrub top he is wearing and his hands are like shovels which have interlocked happily on the desk as he waits for Elsa to speak, looking expectant. Had Elsa met him on the street she would have thought him to be a labourer or rugby player. He looks odd behind his small desk. Like a giant in a doll house.

“Um, yeah. Hi.” Elsa licks her lips quickly to rid herself of the sudden dryness. She crosses her arms defensively over her chest and looks down to the puce coloured countertop, mumbling,” I’m Elsa White. I have an appointment with Dr. Arendelle.”

“Oh ja,” he says happily, large fingers hammering away on his keyboard. “Oh! You’re early.”

Damn fucking empty car park.

Elsa’s nose wrinkles in slight disconcertment and she raises a hand to scratch at an imaginary itch behind her ear. “Yeah,” she sighs. “Is there any way Dr. Arendelle could see me now? I have to get back to work …” she squints at the man’s name tag. ”Oaken, is it?”

“That’s me.” He smiles at her kindly, accent somehow becoming thicker. “Just let me see if one of the nurses can write up a new file. You are a new patient, yes?”

Elsa bobs her head like a seagull who has bashed its head against several lighthouses. Thankfully Oaken doesn’t comment on this strange tick she’s developed and simply begins to dial at his desk phone. He smiles at her as it begins to ring and Elsa tries to return the gesture, looking positively ill.

“Hello, I have a patient here to start a new file and speak to Dr. Arendelle. Ms. White, ja. Early, mhm. Are you available?”

Elsa, who has been wincing repeatedly while Oaken spoke to the faceless and nameless individual over the line, finds that the idea of bolting out of the building and pretending to be a drifter in her little lunch box Fiat from that god awful Tokyo Drift movie is looking like a very feasible option. Before she can recreate some impressive movie skid marks on the lino floor, Oaken hangs up and awards her a giant grin.

“Nurse North will see you in room 5. It’s just passed the waiting room and to your right.”

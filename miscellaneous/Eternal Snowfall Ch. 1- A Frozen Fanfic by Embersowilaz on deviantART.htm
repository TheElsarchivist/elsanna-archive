She couldn't move. It was dark... so dark.. She was freezing, a cold so deep that it burned. The pain was unbearable, but she couldn't open her mouth to scream. Her hands.. she couldn't feel them at all anymore. The absence of pain scared her even more than the jagged tingles shooting throughout the rest of her body. All of a sudden, she couldn't breathe. Her chest aching, she tried as hard as she could to make even the slightest movement. It was hopeless. As black spots danced in her tunnelling vision, she could hear someone scream. 
Anna jolted from the nightmare, and realized she was the one screaming. She quickly slapped her hands over her mouth, her breathing coming in great heaves. Had anyone heard her? God... She hoped not. She had already caused the palace guard night shift to come barrelling into her chambers a week ago. She couldn't stand more embarrassment. Anna waited silently, cold sweat dripping down her back. Nothing moved. Sighing in relief, she lay back down with her arms over her face. These nightmares had to stop. Night after night her mind was plagued by replays of herself freezing solid. Anna didn't blame her sister for what had happened, but... she had actually died, even if it was only for a second. All Anna remembered was throwing herself in front of her sister's crumpled form and the down swipe of Han's sword, then.. nothing. Not until the excruciating pain of her own thawing flesh brought her around. Anna held her hands above her face. Each hand was bandaged individually around each finger and then softly wrapped all together, circling her palm. Even though love had thawed Arendelle, her hands had remained frozen. Almost as a sickly reminder. The frostbite had been so bad, the healer thought Anna would lose at least one finger, but for some reason, they were healing quite nicely. Speedily almost. Must've been the magic. The queen herself didn't even understand her strange power. She had recently been learning to control it, but without too much progress. Her hands still throbbed though. With more pain than Anna would ever let on. Her sister would never forgive herself if she knew the agony Anna was going through. She already punished herself enough for freezing the whole town and Anna's heart. Anna sighed again and sat up, suddenly noticing how cold her room was. A shiver shook through her body. 

"Elsa?" There was no answer. "I know you're there. Come on in," She called softly. 

Her bedroom door slowly opened, revealing her sister. She was wearing a light blue nightgown, coloured like the sky on a crisp winter day. Her long hair cascaded across her shoulders, free from it's usual braid. Her eyebrows were creased with worry. 

"Are you alright?" She asked tentatively. "I heard you yell." 

Anna silently cursed herself for not remembering how close her sister's room was. 

"yeah, I'm alright, just testing the lungs." Anna tried for humour, but Elsa didn't buy it. 

"Was it a bad dream?" 

Anna's silence answered her question. Elsa felt a stab of guilt. This was entirely her fault. She wanted to ask what it had been about, but didn't. Their bond was very loose and Elsa wasn't quite sure how to approach her sister. The years apart from each other had caused an unfixable strain in their relationship. 
There was a long, awkward silence. Elsa turned to leave, feeling uncomfortable. 

"Wait! ... Could... Could you stay?" Anna asked carefully. 

Elsa smiled warmly. Anna had always loved to sleep together, and Elsa was glad that at least that had never changed. 

Elsa walked over to Anna's bed, while Anna slid over and opened the covers. Elsa climbed into the bed, and was surprised when Anna snuggled right up to her and put her arms around her shoulders. 

"I missed you," Anna whispered, her breath tickling Elsa's neck. Elsa wrapped her own arms around her sister as well.

"I missed you too," Elsa said lovingly, feeling Anna sigh slightly and relax into her. They both drifted off to sleep, keeping each other's nightmares at bay.

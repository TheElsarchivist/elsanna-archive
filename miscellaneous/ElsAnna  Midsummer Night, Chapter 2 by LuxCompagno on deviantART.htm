Why did I say that?

Anna leaned against the wall, tears streamed down her face.  Things had been going so well.  Since her resurrection the relationship with her sister had been everything she had dreamed it would be.  

Like the time she had been reading in the library, then dozed off only to be awakened by a distraught Elsa pressing her ear against her chest.  She had kissed away the tears from that beautiful face and reassured Elsa that her heart still beat and only for her.

She loved Elsa so, so much.  Couldn’t she see that?

In her heart of hearts she knew Elsa did; every night, when her sister thought she was asleep she would whisper “I love you, little snowflake” and kiss her on the cheek or forehead.  And even though once, just once, she wished Elsa would say it to her face, it always made her feel loved and protected.

Her sister’s lips were very, very soft.

She didn’t mean to upset Elsa, she really didn’t.  It’s just that sometimes she spoke and acted without thinking first.

… to be honest, maybe it was a little more than sometimes. Okay, maybe it was just a tiny, teeny, teensy bit, just a touch mind, a smidgen more than “sometimes”.

------------------oo0oo----------------------


Why did I say that?

Elsa brushed away angry tears with trembling fingers.

She loved Anna so much but sometimes her sister could be so—

Infuriating.  With no sense of propriety nor decorum.  She sighed.  Maybe that was to be expected; unlike Elsa, who from birth had been practically been raised and prepared to be Monarch of Arendelle, Anna had no such pressure nor obligation.  She could do as she wanted and wore her heart on her sleeve.

Elsa envied, loved and owed her life to that.

She would make it up to Anna tonight, when her sister crept into her bed as she had been doing every night for the past month.  At least she hoped Anna would do that; if Elsa had learned anything from what happened at the Coronation Ball it was they always needed to keep the lines of communications open between them.

Anna, brave fearless Anna, the baby sister who had saved Elsa’s life and said “I love you.”

“Little snowflake, I love you too…”  She bit her lip and whispered, “As more than just a sister.”

She caught her breath; while it might be easy to give voice to that here alone,  Anna could never, ever know.

Conceal, don’t feel.  

Conceal, don’t feel.  

CONCEAL, DON’T FEEL.

It wasn’t working; if anything the frost and ice that coated the room was even thicker than before.

Eternal Snowfall Ch 6 A Frozen Fanfic

  • Literature / Fan Fiction / General Fiction
Night had fallen, and the only source of light in the hut was the glowing embers of the sleeping fire. Elsa lifted her head, grimacing at the tense chords in her neck, sore from leaning against the cot for hours. Glancing around, blinking sleep from her eyes, Elsa remembered what she was doing at Oaken's hut. Snapping around, Elsa saw her sister lying still on the cot. Her eyes were closed gently, freckles scattered along the bridge of her nose, appearing again atop her delicate collarbone, continuing down beneath her loose tunic. Elsa blushed and quickly brought her eyes upwards. Gazing at Anna, Elsa suddenly noticed how flushed the redhead's face looked. Frowning in concern, Elsa laid a cool hand across Anna's forehead. Snatching her hand back from the scorching heat, Elsa stood up and realized that Anna had a serious fever. Remembering a time when she herself had been sick, Elsa recalled that a bad fever could only sweat itself out, and that there wasn't much else one could do for it to get better. Tucking Anna's blanket under the redhead's chin, Elsa went to the hot springs with an empty bucket. Returning hurriedly, Elsa conjured ice cubes to cool the hot water in the now full bucket. Rushing to the closet, Elsa grabbed more blankets and two small washcloths. Bundling Anna up with the extra blankets, the blonde drenched the cloths in the now icy water and wrung it out before placing it on her sister's burning brow. Once that was done, Elsa stoked the dying fire, bringing it back to life, then lay down flush with her sister. Most people assumed that as the Ice Queen Elsa would feel cold to the touch, but in reality Elsa was just as warm as everyone else. She could only make herself cool by forming a slight layer of ice along her body, chilling her skin. Right now, close enough to feel the redhead's breath on her cheek, Elsa banished all thoughts of ice and cold from her mind, and concentrated on getting rid of Anna's fever. 

Many hours later, Elsa, having realized that the cloths on Anna's forehead weren't helping, chilled her own hand and placed it gently against her sister's face. Anna's fever wasn't going away, and Elsa was worried sick for her sister's life. Anna had been in and out of consciousness, eyes rolling and limbs twitching to fight demons unseen by Elsa. Sweeping Anna's sticky, red hair from her face, Elsa noticed that Anna was awake, turquoise eyes burning deep into her own pale blue ones. 

"Hey beautiful," Elsa whispered, clutching at her sister's hot hands. 

"Hey," Anna mumbled, voice crackling from disuse. "Are you okay? You look a bit frantic." 

Tears sprang to Elsa's eyes. Despite being immobile from sickness, Anna could only worry about other people. Forcing a watery smile, Elsa said, "Yeah. Just a little tired. How are you feeling?" 

"Hot. Very hot. Why is it so hot Elsa?" Anna asked, closing her eyes once more. 

"You're very sick. I'm trying to get you better. Don't worry, I won't let anything happen to you. Not ever again," Elsa said, looking away from Anna's face, feeling a stab of guilt for hurting Anna when they were younger, and then once again not too long ago. 

Anna's eyes opened halfway, and fighting exhaustion, the redhead said, "I told you before. That wasn't your fault. Promise me that you will forgive yourself for the accident!" 

Elsa looked back up at her sister and was shocked to see a stubborn glare on the fever-ridden sibling's face. Relieved that Anna was feeling good enough to act stubborn, Elsa smiled slightly. "There's no way I can argue is there?" The unwavering stare of Anna's answered her question. "Okay. You got me. I promise to forgive myself for what I did to you." 

"Finally," Anna breathed, eyes closing when she was satisfied by the blonde's oath. Relaxing into her sister's embrace, Anna fell asleep once more. 

Elsa's eyelids began to droop as well, and cuddling even closer to Anna, the blonde allowed herself the grace of falling into a fitful sleep, wrapping her arms protectively around her younger sibling.

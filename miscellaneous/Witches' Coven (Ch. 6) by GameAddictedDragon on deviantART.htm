"There is no other demon," Vladimir said, approaching the sisters, his stride betraying frustration.

There was something bothering her. Something the seer said was nagging at her. She wracked her brain, trying to grasp what her instincts were trying to tell her.

Her eyes widened. She lashed out, striking Vladimir in his left eye. He stumbled backwards, crying out in pain, covering his eye with his hand.

"Elsa, why'd you do that," Anna snapped, hostility in her voice.

"What's the meaning of this," the man demanded incredulously, shock evidently shown in his uncovered eye.

"The seer didn't say nobody was living here," Elsa snarled. "She said it was abandoned by life. Which means..." She pointed an accusing finger at Vladimir. "It's fair game for the undead."

The accusation hung thickly in the air. A tense silence engulfed the three. The man searched her face, as though looking for any sign that she was joking. But Elsa never wavered. She stood resolutely, never thinking for one second to withdraw her statement.

Vladimir's kindly face morphed into one of wickedness as his cruel laugh resounded around them.

"Ahh, my dear. You certainly are bright." He laughed again, his mouth opened wide to reveal sharp fangs.

"I don't know how you were able to hide it so well," Elsa sneered. "There are usually tell-tale signs that help in identifying a vampire."

He lowered his hand, and glared at Elsa sadistically. She saw that his left eye, the eye she struck, was red now. "Nowadays, it's easy to hide my eyes." He touched the middle of the amethyst of his right eye, making Elsa flinch in disgust. He took away his finger, and his right eye showed itself to be red, while something small and blue stuck to his finger. "Not so much when you knock out my contacts, though," he snipped, squeezing the object between his finger and thumb. It broke with a small crunch.

"Let's go, Anna," Elsa said, preparing an ice spell. Anna didn't respond. "Anna?"

The vampire laughed. "I don't think she'll be doing anything, my dear," he said.

Elsa looked to her sister. Though fire witch had her head bowed, she could see she was smirking. She turned to look at Elsa, who gasped in horror. Her eyes... she looked possessed.

Just as Elsa had said, the demons they're after always attack them on sight. It just so happened that Vladimir had attacked them with an enrapturing spell as soon as he saw them. She wasn't sure why only Anna was affected, but she didn't have time to think about it.

"You bastard," she growled, adopting a hostile stance.

Vladimir's eyes flashed, and Elsa found herself bound with a snare of magic, the binds glowing a devious red. Elsa struggled, trying to free herself.

"I wouldn't do that if I were you," the vampire smirked.

A bolt of magic overtook her. She cried out as pain gripped her entire being. She fell to her knees, Vladimir's laugh resounding around her. She growled. Though her hands were pinned to her sides, she managed to cast gushes of ice at him. He only laughed more.

"Ice doesn't affect me, you fool," he cackled. "Come, my pet," he said to Anna, not removing his gaze from Elsa.

"Yes, Master," Anna said, sensually, approaching the vampire.

"Anna," she exhaled, her voice betraying her hurt. "No..."

A howl in the distance distracted Vladimir. As soon as he broke eye-contact with Elsa, her binds dissolved away. She jumped to her feet and ran to Anna.

"Sorry, Anna," she said. She struck the back of her head. She caught her before she crumpled to the ground. She placed one hand under the backs of Anna's knees, and the other on her shoulder blades. She scooped her up and fled.

"You'll not win this, Witch," Vladimir called after Elsa. "Your sister is mine, now!"
